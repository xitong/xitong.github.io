Research

My main research interest is Information Retrieval, particularly on how to leverage entities (e.g., person, location, organization, concept, etc.) to improve search accuracy. My research topics include:

  • Web Search: Entities have been realized to exist extensively across the Web, and the recent advances of entity recognition techniques make it feasible to mine entities from Web-scale data. We utilize entities in Web search queries and documents as bridge to model the relevance in an entity-based latent space in which additional semantic content could be captured, and therefore it could improve the performance of several state-of-the-art retrieval models significantly.
  • Enterprise Search: Enterprise data is organized in both unstructured and structured format, centering around the employees, products and other entities of the enterprise. Our research leverages the integrated information of entities from unstructured and structured data to deliver better search results.
  • Entity Search: Query log analyses have revealed that a substantial portion of Web search queries target at entities. In stead of retrieving documents in traditional search paradigm, our research explores the entity relations in both unstructured and structured data to retrieve relevant entities to fulfill user's information need directly, therefore saving user's efforts.

Part of my research is supported by HP Labs Innovation Research Program.
